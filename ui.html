After engaging in a conversation with our end user, the physiotherapist, valuable insights were gained, prompting adjustments to our user interface (UI). The physiotherapist emphasized the importance of a graphical representation of data points and suggested replacing the manual generation of graphs through Excel with a more streamlined approach. In response to this feedback, we made the decision to introduce a dedicated "View Charts" page within our UI. This new feature allows users to visually explore various data points over time, providing a more intuitive and efficient way to comprehend trends. By integrating this functionality, we aim to enhance the user experience, ensuring that data analysis is not only comprehensive but also visually accessible, ultimately optimizing the utility of our application for the physiotherapist and facilitating a more informed decision-making process.

Iteration 3

Caption for Image 2

Upon gathering additional insights from the physiotherapist, it became evident that introducing a desired value for each field would significantly enhance the utility of our system. In many instances, physiotherapists seek specific values to gauge a patient's progress, making the inclusion of desired values a crucial feature. To visually reinforce the significance of these desired values, we implemented a red line on the graphs, providing a clear point of reference for assessment and emphasizing the practicality of the graphical representation in identifying trends.

In further discussions with physiotherapists, they highlighted the relevance of LOINC (Logical Observation Identifiers Names and Codes) and SNOMED (Systematized Nomenclature of Medicine) codes in physiotherapy practices, specifically for standardizing measurements and identifying body parts. Acknowledging the importance of aligning with industry conventions, we made the strategic decision to integrate LOINC and SNOMED codes into our system. By doing so, we not only ensure our system is in harmony with established practices but also facilitate seamless communication and interoperability within the physiotherapy community. This integration reflects our commitment to providing a comprehensive and standardized solution tailored to the specific needs of physiotherapists.

Following a discussion with our professor, it became evident that there was an opportunity to refine the user-centricity of our system. The suggestion to incorporate both tabular and graphical views for presenting data emerged as a key enhancement. By providing users with the flexibility to seamlessly toggle between these two perspectives, we empower them with greater control over the system. This dual-view functionality not only caters to diverse user preferences but also acknowledges the varying analytical needs that users may have. The tabular view offers a detailed, structured representation of data, while the graphical view ensures a visual, at-a-glance comprehension of trends. This integration places user control and choice at the forefront, ultimately enriching the overall user experience within our system.

After revisiting our users to present the refinements we integrated into our sketches, we identified a potential concern with the existing design. The amalgamation of different components, including the latest consultation, today's consultation, and the management of desired SNOMED values, all confined to a single page, resulted in an overflow of content.Further resulting in an inconvenient amount of scrolling. To address this concern and enhance user control, we have implemented tabs for each distinct section of our system. This strategic adjustment ensures that users can efficiently navigate between sections without the need for continuous scrolling, thereby improving the overall usability of the interface.

By introducing these tabs, we not only mitigate the scrolling issue but the improved navigational structure aligns with the users' mental models, providing a more intuitive and predictable experience. Users can now easily locate and access specific sections, fostering a sense of control and reducing cognitive load.This modification contributes to a cleaner and less cluttered interface, promoting faster information retrieval and aids in preventing information overload. As a result, the users can focus more on the task at hand, enhancing their overall efficiency and satisfaction with the system.

The landing page of our system is a “find patient profile” page where the user needs to enter the patient ID. When the user clicks on “next”, the user lands on the dashboard page. This has the required details of the patient that the physiotherapists need to see such as name, date of birth and patient ID. Then there is a display of the “Latest consultation” where the details of the previous consultation. When “show more past data” is clicked, the page below appears. Through this page, the physiotherapist can find all the past data of all previous consultations, filtering which specific consultations and SNOMED codes they want to view. The physiotherapist is able to download all the details of a specific consultation which includes the notes and values recorded during the consultation. In order to return back to the page above, click “back to dashboard”. When the user clicks on download full data of specific consultation, a table will be downloaded like the one below. The table will contain all the values recorded in that specific consultation with the notes of that consultation there too. In the dashboard page, the user is able to switch between a tabular and graphical view of the data. In the graphical view, the user can select a specific SNOMED code they want to observe the trend of through consultations. This graphical view enables the user to visually see progress made through the ability to see the desired value against the values recorded during each session. As the user scrolls down the dashboard page, they will see the “Today’s consultation section. Here the physiotherapist is able to add in the problem description, which are qualitative notes they write during the consultation. They are then able to add new measure values of the current consultation, adding as many as required. For each value they also need to find out the SNOMED and LOINC codes. After they are done, the user needs to click save. With the bin icon, the user needs to click it to remove a row of a value that they don’t require. The add icon is to add another row, so they can add in more values they’ve recorded to the system. The final section of the dashboard page is the updating of desired SNOMED values. Here there is a table of all the SNOMED codes that the current patient is seen for and their corresponding desired values. The physiotherapist is able to update the desired value and progress is not linear and usually the milestones reached in treatment can sometimes change the desired values. When the user clicks “log out” they return back to the “Find patient profile”. This is the full proposed lifecycle of the system.

User Interface Design

The User Interface (UI) design for our physiotherapy system is a culmination of thoughtful application of established design principles aimed at providing physiotherapists with a tool that is not only powerful but also intuitive and efficient to use. Here's a detailed justification of our design choices:

  • Flexibility: The user interface has been meticulously crafted to empower users with the flexibility to customize their system experience according to their preferences. Whether opting for a visually intuitive graph representation or a more structured tabular view, users can effortlessly navigate and interpret the data from physio sessions in a way that suits them best. In the initial stages, our design inclination leaned towards a singular reliance on the graph view, supplemented by the provision of Excel sheets for each SNOMED code. However, upon revisiting this approach, we recognized the potential cognitive overload posed by the multitude of pages, particularly with the extensive data also available in the Excel sheets. To enhance user clarity and streamline the system, we opted for a tabular view. This user-friendly interface allows users to conveniently filter data based on SNOMED values, facilitating a more focused and efficient exploration of information. Additionally, we introduced a "recent consultation details" feature to further enhance user accessibility and overall ease of interaction with the system.
  • Simplicity: We've chosen a simple and minimalistic UI, prioritizing clarity and ease of use. This intentional design approach ensures that our UI remains intuitive and user-friendly, allowing individuals to navigate the system effortlessly. By minimizing complexity and focusing on essential features, we aim to enhance the overall user experience, making interactions with our system straightforward and enjoyable. The straightforward presentation of patient data, with clear labels for SNOMED codes and values, is an example of this principle in action.
  • Consistency: Consistency in design creates a more predictable and understandable experience. Throughout the interface, consistent color schemes, button styles, and typography are used. This consistency is visible in the uniformity of interactive elements like dropdowns and buttons ("Tabular View" and "Graphical View" toggles), which follow the same design patterns, reducing the cognitive load for users as they navigate through different parts of the system.
  • Effective Feedback / Visibility: Effective feedback is crucial for keeping users informed about the success of their actions. In our system, we prioritize this by implementing a prompt system. For instance, when a user adds new consultation details and saves them, a pop-up message instantly appears, confirming that the "session saved successfully." This immediate and reactive feedback enhances user confidence in the system, fostering a more comfortable and trusting user experience. Moreover, the feedback in our system is visually accessible, allowing users to witness and validate the impact of their changes instantly.
  • Feedback: Feedback is essential to interact with any system. Our UI provides immediate feedback upon user interaction. For example, when a physiotherapist selects a different view or a specific SNOMED code, the system responds instantly by updating the display accordingly. This immediate response lets users know their actions have been registered.
  • Tolerance: Our system is tolerant of errors, providing easy ways to correct them. The ability to easily switch between "Tabular" and "Graphical" views allows users to navigate between detailed data and visual representations without penalty or confusion, accommodating different preferences or accidental clicks.
  • Affordance: Elements are designed so that they are intuitively understood in terms of their functionality. For instance, buttons for downloading data are placed right next to the data points they relate to, making it clear what action will be taken when they are clicked.
  • User Centric Design: The design is tailored for physiotherapists who need quick access to patient data and trends. The graphical view, for instance, provides an immediate visual representation of a patient’s progress over time, which is a vital feature for tracking rehabilitation outcomes.
  • Accessibility: Accessibility features are incorporated to ensure that the UI is usable by all physiotherapists, including those with disabilities. Adequate color contrast, as shown in the screenshots, ensures readability for users with visual impairments.
  • Efficiency: Efficiency is key for busy professionals. The "Show More Past Data" button is a direct call to action that provides more detailed historical data on demand, allowing physiotherapists to quickly access extended patient histories when needed without navigating away from the dashboard.
  • Placing the user at the centre: Placing users at the core of every UI design decision is of paramount importance. Following the creation of each sketch, we consistently circle back to our users, actively seeking their valuable input and gaining an in-depth understanding of their needs. This iterative process ensures that our design choices are not only aligned with user preferences but also rooted in a profound comprehension of their requirements. By prioritizing user feedback throughout the design journey, we cultivate a user-centric approach that enhances the usability of the interface.
  • Constraints/Error Prevention: Emphasis on precision extends to the careful imposition of character constraints on SNOMED codes, LOINC codes, and associated values within our system. These constraints play a pivotal role in error prevention, ensuring a seamless flow of operations for both the system and the user. By limiting the acceptable character types of SNOMED and LOINC codes, as well as their respective values, we fortify the reliability of our system. This approach not only safeguards against potential inaccuracies but also contributes to an enhanced user experience, promoting a system that operates efficiently and with minimal disruptions.

The blue color theme is calm and professional, aligning with healthcare settings, while the ample white space prevents information overload. The layout is strategically organized, with the most frequently used information placed prominently for easy access, facilitating a smooth workflow for physiotherapists who would be using the system in potentially fast-paced environments.
