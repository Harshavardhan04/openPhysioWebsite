FHIR

As of now, the use of FHIR specifically within physiotherapy applications is not as prominently highlighted or well-documented as in other medical specialties. However, the principles of FHIR's application in physiotherapy align closely with its use in broader healthcare contexts, aiming to enhance interoperability and patient data management. Integrating into the broader medical context, FHIR's impact is more evident in various specialties through specific systems and applications. For example, in oncology, the mCODE (Minimal Common Oncology Data Elements) initiative uses FHIR to standardize data for cancer care, facilitating better data sharing and patient management. In the realm of mental health, applications like the Cerner Behavioral Health EHR leverage FHIR to integrate diverse patient data sources, enhancing care coordination and treatment planning. Moreover, in the context of general healthcare, systems like Epic and Cerner, as previously mentioned, employ FHIR extensively. Epic's Share Everywhere feature, for instance, uses FHIR to enable patients to grant doctors outside the Epic system temporary access to their health records, directly benefiting patient care continuity. While these do not specifically reference physiotherapy, they underscore the versatility and potential of FHIR to transform data handling in various medical specialties, including physiotherapy, as the field advances and adopts more digital health technologies.

Having had a research on some existing systems which use FHIR, we evaluate a good FHIR-compliant system for physiotherapy by the following features:

  • Comprehensive Interoperability: The system effectively uses FHIR standards to facilitate seamless and secure exchange of healthcare information across different healthcare systems, applications, and stakeholders. This includes the ability to accurately interpret, exchange, and use patient data in various formats from multiple sources.
  • Scalability: A well-implemented FHIR system is scalable, capable of handling increasing data volumes and connections with new data sources or healthcare entities without compromising performance or data integrity.Prompt and correct feedbacks to users' needs
  • User-centric Design: The system is designed with end-users in mind, whether they are healthcare providers, patients, or administrators. This means providing intuitive interfaces, ensuring data is presented in a meaningful and accessible manner, and enabling users to perform their tasks efficiently and effectively.
  • Compliance and Standardization: The system fully adheres to the FHIR specifications and is regularly updated to align with the latest version of the standard. It ensures that the data models, resources, and APIs are used as intended to maintain compatibility and interoperability with other FHIR-based systems.

FHIR Resources in Development

In the development of our project, we have integrated Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources (FHIR) to streamline and enhance data exchange across different healthcare platforms. FHIR serves as a robust framework, providing a set of standardized resources that can be used and combined to solve a wide range of health-related problems. It simplifies the process of healthcare data integration by providing a common language and set of rules to ensure that the data is both machine-readable and human-friendly.

The flexibility of FHIR resources allows us to develop scalable applications that can adapt to the evolving needs of healthcare providers and patients. Using resources such as Patient, Observation, and Encounter, we can create detailed clinical records and enable real-time data retrieval, which is crucial for effective patient management and care coordination.

Moreover, the modular nature of FHIR resources facilitates developers in customizing and extending functionalities as per project requirements. Our use of FHIR has enabled us to ensure interoperability across different systems, leading to more cohesive and seamless workflows. This has been particularly advantageous in scenarios where quick access to accurate and comprehensive patient information is critical.

Through leveraging FHIR, we aim to not only meet current healthcare standards but also pave the way for future innovations in healthcare technology.

Backend Decision: Python Over Java

In our pursuit of selecting an optimal programming language for the backend development, we gravitated towards Python over Java after a comprehensive evaluation. Python's inherent simplicity and its robust library ecosystem make it particularly advantageous for our project's requirements, which include extensive data manipulation and seamless API integration. These capabilities are critical, given our project's need to interface with the NHS's EPIC system and other healthcare data sources. Moreover, Python's widespread adoption in the healthcare sector offers an additional layer of community support and resources, which we find invaluable. While Java does boast more extensive documentation, our team's proficiency in Python and its faster development cycle promise a more streamlined and efficient project timeline, aligning perfectly with our project's objectives and time constraints.

Backend Framework Decision: Flask Over Other Options

We chose Flask as our backend framework due to its lightweight and modular nature, fitting perfectly with our need for a flexible and efficient backend. Flask's ability to easily handle HTTP requests and responses aligns with our use of Axios for making asynchronous HTTP requests from the frontend. This combination allows for efficient data fetching and manipulation, supporting our application's dynamic content. Flask's simplicity and scalability complement Axios' promise-based structure, enabling us to build a responsive and maintainable backend system. The decision was also influenced by Flask's well-documented nature and the robust support from the Python community, ensuring we have the resources needed for effective problem-solving and implementation.

Frontend Decision: React Over HTML/CSS and Vue.js

Transitioning to the frontend, our decision to adopt React over traditional HTML/CSS or even Vue.js was informed by several strategic considerations. React's component-based architecture allows us to build a more dynamic, interactive user experience, crucial for the modern web applications we aim to develop. Its rich ecosystem and the availability of numerous third-party libraries mean we can implement advanced functionalities and maintain high performance without compromising on quality. Our team's existing familiarity with React significantly reduces the learning curve and accelerates development, ensuring we meet our project milestones efficiently. While Vue.js is a commendable framework with its own strengths, React's broader adoption, coupled with its comprehensive suite of features like Hooks for state management and side effects, makes it a more fitting choice for our project's ambitious goals.

Moreover, when it comes to data visualization, we opted for Chart.js over other graph generation libraries or plain HTML/CSS due to its seamless integration with React, extensive documentation, and wide range of customizable chart options. Chart.js provides a responsive and aesthetically pleasing way to display our data, which is essential for physiotherapist engagement and comprehension. Its performance and ease of use in dynamically updating and rendering charts align perfectly with the interactive nature of our application, making it an indispensable tool in our frontend development toolkit.

The Significance of JSON in FHIR

JSON (JavaScript Object Notation) files are crucial in FHIR (Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources) for various reasons that align with FHIR's objectives of enhancing interoperability, efficiency, and accessibility in healthcare data exchange. Below are key points highlighting the importance of JSON in FHIR:

  • Human Readability and Machine Parsability: JSON's format is easily understandable by humans and machines, facilitating clear and accurate healthcare data handling.
  • Interoperability: The wide support of JSON across different platforms ensures seamless data exchange between diverse healthcare systems.
  • Web and Mobile-Friendly: JSON's integration with web technologies aligns with FHIR's support for modern healthcare applications, enhancing its utility in web-based and mobile environments.
  • Efficiency: The compactness of JSON files promotes faster data processing and reduced bandwidth usage, crucial for medical applications' performance.
  • Flexibility: JSON's versatile structure can represent various healthcare information, adapting to FHIR's diverse data representation needs.
  • Standardization and Tool Support: JSON's established standards and extensive tooling facilitate the development and maintenance of FHIR-compliant applications.

These attributes make JSON an integral component of FHIR, ensuring the standard meets its goals in healthcare information exchange.
