Critical Evaluation

User Experience and user interface:

We have implemented user testing as a fundamental aspect of our design process to ensure that our system is not only intuitive but also enjoyable for our end users. By doing so, we were able to identify redundancies and potential areas of confusion in our initial UI design. Through constant iteration driven by user feedback and adherence to good HCI principles, we have successfully crafted a highly user-friendly web application.

Our system provides users with the capability to input both quantitative and qualitative data for each session, effectively digitizing all aspects of their paper-based documentation process. However, had we been aware of the SOAP convention commonly used by physiotherapists for session recording earlier in our development process, we could have integrated its elements to enhance the user experience with our application. While users can currently utilize the "problem description" textbox to adhere to the SOAP writing convention, implementing separate text boxes for subjective, objective, assessment, and plan components for each session would have allowed for a more structured and intuitive experience. This segmentation would enable users to easily view and manage each aspect of their session documentation separately, ultimately enhancing their overall satisfaction with our system.

Our decision to utilize tabs for navigating various elements within the system empowers users, instilling a sense of control and facilitating seamless usage. The overall flow of the system has been carefully crafted to be inherently intuitive. We intentionally opted for a simplistic UI approach, akin to the NHS system, to facilitate smooth integration with existing NHS systems in the future. This approach ensures that our system maintains enough familiarity with users accustomed to NHS interfaces, thus promoting seamless integration.

In summary, our system is primarily focused on providing physiotherapists with graphical data representation and centralized access to all relevant notes and values. Through our efforts, we have successfully realized this goal, empowering physiotherapists with a comprehensive and user-friendly tool tailored to their needs.

Project Management

The project management approach was executed with precision and efficiency. At the outset, we implemented a comprehensive Gantt chart, providing visibility into the workload of each team member and ensuring alignment with project deadlines. In addition to the Gantt chart, we leveraged the capabilities of the work management platform Asana, enabling seamless task addition, delegation, and prioritization. This integration significantly enhanced our team's productivity and coordination throughout the project lifecycle.

Furthermore, maintaining open communication channels, we regularly convened with both clients and end-users monthly. These meetings served as invaluable opportunities to report progress, address any concerns or uncertainties, and gather feedback to refine our deliverables further. This proactive engagement strategy not only fostered a strong sense of collaboration but also ensured that our project remained aligned with stakeholder expectations and requirements throughout its development stages.

Functionality

We have successfully completed all core requirements outlined for this project. Every must-have and should-have requirement has been fulfilled, ensuring a robust foundation for our system. Additionally, we have achieved two-thirds of the could-have requirements, significantly enhancing the comprehensiveness of our solution. This comprehensive coverage reflects our dedication to delivering a holistic system. Our team has demonstrated exceptional proficiency in implementing all functionalities expected and desired by the user.

Stability

Our web application is stable, as evidenced by rigorous testing of the system through unit tests and UAT. We have enlisted multiple end users to evaluate the final system, and they have consistently reported excellent response times and encountered no system errors during their testing. Yet, while these individual tests demonstrate its stability, we have not yet assessed how the system performs under the load of multiple users simultaneously. Load testing remains pending, preventing us from offering a complete evaluation of the system's stability.

Compatibility

Our web application offers exceptional compatibility across various devices, making it accessible on tablets, desktops, and laptops—a valuable asset in the healthcare domain. An enhancement we envision is deploying our application as a downloadable app on both Android and Apple app stores. This would facilitate physiotherapists who conduct home visits to access crucial patient information conveniently via their smartphones, streamlining their workflow.

Moreover, our system is fully compatible with the NHS infrastructure, enabling seamless integration with the current Electronic Health Record (EHR) system, due to the utilization of Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources (FHIR).

maintainability

The code base of our application is documented and adheres closely to FHIR standards, ensuring clarity and simplicity in its structure. This emphasis on comprehensive documentation and adherence to industry standards significantly enhances the maintainability of our system. Furthermore, the simplicity of our codebase not only facilitates easier understanding for developers but also streamlines the debugging and troubleshooting processes.

Future Work

Given that we met all of our client’s requirements, the only future work would be to expand the project. The entire project was designed with generalisation and expansion in mind. The main future potential of the project is to introduce visual computing through body tracking softwares such as OpenCV and MotionPipe. For example, measuring the angle that the forearm makes with the upper arm during bicep flexion. Softwares that can easily implement such body tracking have been published openly.

This would allow patients to bring quantitative explanations of how they found the exercises that physios give them at home by tracking the joint angles and automatically storing them using our software. The implication of this is that physios would need less time to understand the patients description of how they’ve progressed since their last consultation. Another implication of this would be the improvement of distanced treatment such as during a pandemic, a patient could potentially have a consultation over a web cam and not have to be physically there for a physio to take angle measurements. This is highly revelant especially considering recent global events.
